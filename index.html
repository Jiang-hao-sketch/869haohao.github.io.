Abstract

In the field of dual-arm collaborative robotic manipulation, balancing task precision and computational efficiency is crucial. However, existing diffusion policies struggle to achieve this balance due to limited model expressiveness and inefficient iterative sampling. To address this, we propose HF-M2C (Hierarchical Flow Matching with Mamba2-Cross-Attention), an action generation policy framework specifically designed for dual-arm manipulation. This framework achieves breakthroughs through three core designs: employing FiLM mechanisms for task-conditional modulation of the visual encoder; introducing Mamba2 state space models working synergistically with Cross-Attention to strengthen sequential modeling of dual-arm action dependencies; and utilizing conditional flow matching to regress deterministic velocity fields, directly learning action evolution trajectories to simplify training and significantly reduce dependence on iterative denoising. Extensive experiments on the RoboTwin2 benchmark demonstrate that HF-M2C achieves superior performance across multiple synchronous and asynchronous dual-arm manipulation tasks while maintaining high real-time inference efficiency.
